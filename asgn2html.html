Life Expectancy & Population - Japan

Life Expctancy is the statistical measure of the average time an organism is expected to live based on the year of its birth. It has been varyinig since the beginning of life. Japan is well-known for having the highest life expectancy among the G7 countries, yet in the early 1960s, Japanese life expectancy was the lowest of any G7 country. It is believed that the change in diet and several other factors improved their life expectancy. Thus, we can see differences in average life expectancy in different prefectures of Japan, due to the difference in lifestyle.

Life Expectancy by Prefectures

The overall average life expectancy is 83.89. As observed, Akita and Aomori have the lowest life expectancy, and they are both in the Tohoku(Northeastern) Region. On the other hand, Shiga, Nagano and Fukui have the highest life expectancy, and three of them are located in the Chubu(Central) Region, with Shiga a little towards the west. These data convey something about the difference in how people live in different region, and how it affects their life expectancy.
